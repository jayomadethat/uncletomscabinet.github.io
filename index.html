Enslaved Child Reads Word, South Declares It 'Witchcraft'

May 27, 1852

In what plantation overseers are calling "a frightful act of literary voodoo," an enslaved child shocked the community by allegedly reading a word aloud from a discarded Bible. Local authorities were quick to denounce the act as witchcraft, citing the child’s "unnatural gaze" and the suspicious presence of literacy.

“Next thing you know, they’ll want shoes, too.” — Concerned Citizen

Ministers gathered to perform a ritual burning of all pamphlets, books, and northern newspapers within a 10-mile radius.

Cotton Plantation Suffers Catastrophic Loss: Slave Breaks Toe, Delays Harvest by 6 Minutes

May 30, 1852

In a devastating blow to Southern productivity, a field slave at Magnolia Grove Plantation stubbed his toe on a cotton root Tuesday morning, resulting in a six-minute delay in harvest operations. Planters across the region are calling it “the most disruptive act of disobedience since rain,” with one overseer demanding congressional hearings on “toenail sabotage.”
